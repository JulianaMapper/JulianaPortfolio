Personal Brand Style Brief

Juliana McMillan-Wilhoit — "Balanced" direction (Still Water palette)

v1.0 — February 2026

Overview

Brand positioning

Visual Personality

Professional with warmth. Structured with joy. Clear enough to scan in 8 seconds, human enough to remember.

This brand reads "product leader" first and "interesting human" second. The green accent carries competence, growth, and your public health roots. The coral appears only as a highlight, never as a structural color. It's the joy. Light backgrounds keep the focus on content and case studies. Typography is dyslexia-friendly throughout: even stroke widths, open letterforms, generous spacing.

This brand feels like

A well-organized portfolio on a clean desk. The person who owns it clearly thinks in systems. There's a plant on the windowsill.

This brand does not feel like

A moody designer portfolio. A startup landing page with gradients. A government form. A Pinterest mood board.

01 — Color

Do

Use coral (#D4A07A) as an inline highlight tint behind key phrases. Use it in warm tags and a tertiary button option. Let it appear in small moments that feel like joy.

Avoid

Using coral as a background color, section divider, or primary button. It competes with the green when given too much structural weight. Coral is the seasoning, not the plate.

02 — Typography

Type system

Two font families. DM Sans handles all content (headlines and body). Outfit handles all interface elements (labels, buttons, tags, navigation, metadata). Both are dyslexia-friendly: even stroke widths, open counters, distinct letterforms (I/l/1 are easily distinguishable), tall x-height.

Content Font — DM Sans

DM Sans

Headlines (700), subheads (500), body text (400). Slightly softer geometry than geometric sans-serifs like Sora. Warm curves carry approachability without sacrificing clarity. Use for all long-form content, hero headlines, case study narratives.

Interface Font — Outfit

Outfit

Section labels (600), buttons (600), tags (500), navigation (500), metadata (400). Clean geometric construction provides clear hierarchy distinction from DM Sans. Use for all UI chrome, wayfinding, and structural elements.

Readability Guidelines

Line length: Maximum 680px (roughly 70-75 characters per line). Wider lines strain tracking for all readers.

Paragraph spacing: Use var(--space-4) (1rem / 16px) between paragraphs. Avoid relying on first-line indent.

Text alignment: Left-aligned only. Never justify. Ragged right edges help readers with dyslexia find the next line.

Bold usage: DM Sans 700 for headlines, 600 for card heads. In body copy, use 700 sparingly for key phrases (strong tags). Avoid italic in body text.

All-caps: Outfit only. Used exclusively for section labels, eyebrows, and button text. Never apply all-caps to DM Sans or body copy.

Common Spacing Patterns

Page padding: 1.5rem horizontal (--space-6), 2.5rem vertical (--space-10). On mobile, maintain horizontal padding at 1.5rem minimum.

Between sections: 4rem (--space-16). Major content sections need breathing room.

Section label to section title: 0.5rem (--space-2).

Section title to content: 1.5rem (--space-6).

Card internal padding: 1.5rem (--space-6) for standard cards. 1rem/1.5rem (--space-4/--space-6) for compact cards.

Between cards: 1rem (--space-4).

Tag/button gap: 0.5rem (--space-2) between tags. 0.75rem (--space-3) between buttons.

Paragraph spacing: 1rem (--space-4) between paragraphs within body text.

06 — Layout

Layout principles

Grid & Containers

Max content width: 1080px. Centered with auto margins.

Max text width: 680px. Applied to body copy and descriptions.

Card grids: CSS Grid, 1fr columns. 2-col for comparisons, 3-col for stats. Gap: 1rem. Collapse to 1-col at 768px.

Section flow: section-label → section-title → section-note (optional) → content. Consistent rhythm throughout.

08 — Brand Voice

How this pairs with Jefferson

Jefferson's system: Plus Jakarta Sans, charcoal background (#18191E), purple accent. Sharp, executive, CIO energy.

Juliana's system: DM Sans + Outfit, warm cream ground (#F7F5F0), green accent (#376E5A) with coral highlight (#D4A07A). Structured, warm, product leader energy.

Together: Complementary, not matching. Dark/light. Purple/green. Both read as senior leadership. Neither looks like the other's template. If placed side by side, they look like co-founders with distinct strengths — which is exactly what they are.
