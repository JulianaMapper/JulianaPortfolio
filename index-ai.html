Most AI tools fail not because the technology is wrong — but because nobody listened to the people who would use them. I've built AI products for public health departments, the U.S. military, and Fortune 500 supply chains. Every time, the work started the same way: I went to the people first.

I'm not an AI evangelist. I'm interested in where AI actually fits — and where organizations aren't ready for it yet. That gap between "what the tool can do" and "what the team can absorb" is where I do my best work.
